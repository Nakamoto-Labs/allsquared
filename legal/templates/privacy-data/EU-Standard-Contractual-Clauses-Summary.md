# EU Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) - Summary & Reference

**Official Source:** Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914

The SCCs are a standardized set of contractual terms approved by the European Commission for transfers of personal data from the EU/EEA to third countries.

## Four Modules

### Module 1: Controller-to-Controller (C2C)
Transfer between two data controllers.

### Module 2: Controller-to-Processor (C2P)
Transfer from a data controller to a data processor (most common for SaaS).

### Module 3: Processor-to-Processor (P2P)
Transfer from a data processor to a sub-processor.

### Module 4: Processor-to-Controller (P2C)
Transfer from a data processor back to a data controller (rare).

## Key Clauses

1. **Purpose and scope** - Identification of data transfers
2. **Data protection safeguards** - Technical and organizational measures
3. **Rights of data subjects** - Right to obtain copy, erasure, rectification
4. **Obligations of the data importer** - Processing restrictions, sub-processing rules
5. **Remedies** - Complaint mechanisms, liability
6. **Supervision** - DPA oversight mechanisms
7. **Termination** - Conditions for ending the transfer arrangement

## Annexes Required
- **Annex I:** List of parties, description of transfer, competent supervisory authority
- **Annex II:** Technical and organizational measures (TOMs)
- **Annex III:** List of sub-processors (if applicable)

## CCPA/CPRA Service Provider Addendum
California Consumer Privacy Act requires service providers to:
- Process personal information only as specified in the agreement
- Not sell or share the personal information
- Not combine PI with data from other sources
- Allow the business to monitor compliance
- Notify the business if unable to meet obligations
