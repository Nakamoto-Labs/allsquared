# Appointment and Term

### The Company appoints the Advisor to act as advisor, and provide assistance, to the Company. The details of the services to be provided by the Advisor are set out in Schedule 2 (the **Services**). 

### This Agreement starts on the Commencement Date as set out in Item 3 of Schedule 1 and continues until terminated.

# Services

## Advisor responsibilities

### In relation to the provision of the Services, the Advisor must: 

#### ensure that the Services are performed in a lawful, competent, timely and professional manner and with all due care, skill and diligence;

#### promote the Company's business, interests and reputation;

#### not commit any act or omission which directly or indirectly may cause damage to the Company's business, interests or reputation;

#### comply with all applicable laws and regulations and policies and procedures of the Company; and

#### act in good faith in all of the Advisor's dealings with the Company.

## Nature of Relationship

### The Parties acknowledge and agree that in providing the Services, the Advisor acts as an independent contractor and not as an employee, partner, joint venture party or agent of the Company and that the Advisor is solely responsible for controlling the manner in which the Services are provided.

### The Advisor must not:

#### act for, incur any obligations on behalf of, or bind the Company in any manner whatsoever; or

#### represent itself, in any manner, whether directly or indirectly, as being a director, officer, employee or agent of the Company.

### The Advisor is responsible for the payment of wages, superannuation, insurance and taxation for their staff, and for its own workers' compensation insurance and other insurance liabilities. 

# Contract Fee and Expenses

## Contract Fee

> The Company will grant the Advisor the equity compensation as set out in Item 5 of Schedule 1 within 30 days of the Commencement Date. The Company will not pay the Advisor any additional fee in relation to the Services.

## Costs and Expenses

### The Company will reimburse the Advisor for reasonable out of pocket expenses incurred in providing the Services, for which prior written approval has been obtained from the Company. {#the-company-will-reimburse-the-advisor-for-reasonable-out-of-pocket-expenses-incurred-in-providing-the-services-for-which-prior-written-approval-has-been-obtained-from-the-company. .unnumbered}

# Intellectual Property

## Intellectual Property 

### The Advisor must promptly disclose to the Company all Company Intellectual Property.

### To the extent not otherwise vested in the Company, the Advisor irrevocably assigns to the Company its entire right, title, interest in and to all Company Intellectual Property including all ownership rights, copyright, and the exclusive right to develop, make, use, sell, license, adapt, change, alter or otherwise benefit from such Company Intellectual Property.

### The Advisor must execute on demand all documents and take all steps necessary to ensure the Company secures the best protection and/or rights relating to such Company Intellectual Property in Australia and/or anywhere in the world. 

### The Advisor warrants and agrees that the Company Intellectual Property is or will be original and use of the Company Intellectual Property by the Company will not infringe the intellectual property rights of any person or other rights or laws.

## Moral Rights

> To the full extent permitted under the *Copyright Act 1968* (Cth), by signing this Agreement the Advisor:

### voluntarily and unconditionally consents to all or any acts or omissions by the Company, or persons authorised by the Company, concerning any and all Works made or to be made by the Advisor (whether before or after this consent is given) in the course of providing the Services which would otherwise infringe the Advisor's Moral Rights;

### waives any and all existing and future Moral Rights in the Works; and

### acknowledges that it has given this consent voluntarily and without reliance on any statement or representation made by the Company, or anyone acting on the Company's behalf.

# Confidential Information

### The Advisor acknowledges that it may be given access to or obtain Confidential Information in the course of providing the Services and agrees that Confidential Information is of considerable commercial value to the Company. 

### The Advisor must, at all times whilst providing the Services and thereafter: 

#### not disclose Confidential Information to a third party, except in the proper course of providing the Services;

#### not use Confidential Information for any other purpose other than for the Company's benefit; and

#### keep confidential, maintain proper and secure custody of and not use, disclose, publish or reproduce, in any form (including via verbal discussion), any Confidential Information except as authorised by the Company, reasonably required to provide the Services or required by law. 

# Privacy

### The privacy obligations in this clause 7 apply to all personal information (as defined in the *Privacy Act 1988* (Cth)) (**Personal Information**) that the Advisor (or its employees) receives from the Company (for any reason, and whether directly or indirectly) or which the Advisor (or its employees) receives, creates or holds in connection with the Services and/or this Agreement.

### The Advisor acknowledges and agrees that the Company owns all such Personal Information.

### The Advisor must handle, and must ensure that its employees handle, all Personal Information in accordance with the Privacy Act including the Australian Privacy Principles.

### This clause 7 applies regardless of whether the Personal Information, the Advisor and/or any third party to whom the Advisor discloses Personal Information is located in or outside the State set out in Item 4 of Schedule 1 (the **State**).

# Conflict of Interest or Duty

### The Advisor must not, without the prior written consent of the Company, accept or perform any work or engage in any activity which, in the Company's opinion, is or may reasonably be seen to be: 

#### in conflict with the provision of the Services to the Company; or

#### in conflict with or adverse to the Company's activities, affairs or reputation.

### If provision of the Services results in, or is reasonably likely to result in, a conflict of interest or duty with any other work the Advisor is performing or has agreed to perform for another person, the Advisor must, immediately on becoming aware of the conflict or reasonable likelihood of the conflict:

#### notify the Company; and

#### stop performing the other work or terminate the agreement to perform the other work which has given rise, or will give rise, to the conflict.

# Termination and Suspension

## Mutual termination by notice

### Either Party may terminate this Agreement by giving the other Party \[[14]{.mark}\] days' written notice.  {#either-party-may-terminate-this-agreement-by-giving-the-other-party-14-days-written-notice. .unnumbered}

## Obligations on termination

### On termination of this Agreement, the Advisor must:

#### stop performing the Services; and

#### immediately return to the Company all property (including Confidential Information and Company Intellectual Property) in its possession that belongs to the Company.

## Survival

> Clauses 1, 6, 9, 10, 11, 13 and Schedule 1 survive termination or expiry of this Agreement.

# General

## Subcontracting

> The Advisor must not subcontract the performance of any part of the Services without the prior written consent of the Company.

## Notices

### A notice or other communication given under this Agreement must be in writing and addressed and delivered to the intended recipient by hand, prepaid post or email in accordance with the notice details last notified by the recipient to the Parties.

### A Party's notice details are set out in items 1 and 2 of Schedule 1. A Party may change its notice details by written notice to the other Parties. 

## Severance

### If a provision of this Agreement is held to be void, invalid, illegal or unenforceable, that provision must be read down as narrowly as necessary to allow it to be valid or enforceable.

### If it is not possible to read down a provision (in whole or in part), that provision (or that part of that provision) is severed from this Agreement without affecting the validity or enforceability of the remainder of that provision or the other provisions in this Agreement.

## Entire agreement

> This Agreement contains the entire understanding between the Parties, and supersedes all previous discussions, communications, negotiations, understandings, representations, warranties, commitments and agreements, in respect of its subject matter.

## Amendment

> This Agreement may only be amended by written document executed by all Parties.

## Governing law and jurisdiction

> This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State. Each Party irrevocably and unconditionally submits to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts operating in the State and any courts entitled to hear appeals from those courts and waives any right to object to proceedings being brought in those courts.

# Definitions and Interpretation

## Definitions

> In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires:
>
> **Company Intellectual Property** means all works, inventions, discoveries, improvements, designs, computer programs, processes, concepts, strategies and plans that are conceived, created or developed by the Advisor (or any person authorised to provide the Services on the Advisor's behalf) whilst providing the Services which have been, are or become relevant to a Group Company. This includes:

### copyright;

a.  registered or unregistered design, patent, trade mark rights;

b.  trade, business, company or domain names;

c.  know-how, inventions, processes, trade secrets, Confidential Information;

d.  circuit layouts, databases or source codes; or

e.  similar rights in any part of the world,

> relating to a Group Company and/or the operation of its business, including any application, or right to apply, for registration of, and any improvements, enhancements or modifications of, the foregoing.
>
> **Confidential Information** means all information (whether or not it is marked or described as confidential) in any form or medium concerning any past, present or future business, operations or affairs of a Group Company, or any of its customers, including:

### all technical or non-technical data, formulae, patterns, programs, devices, methods, techniques, plans, drawings, models and processes, source and object code, software and computer records;

### all business and marketing plans and projections, details of agreements and arrangements with third parties, and customer and supplier information and lists;

### all financial information, pricing schedules and structures, product margins, contract fees and remuneration details and investment outlays; 

### all information concerning any employee, customer, contractor, supplier or agent of the Group; 

### the Company's policies and procedures, if any; and

### all information contained in or produced under or in connection with this Agreement,

### but excludes information that is in the public domain (other than by way of unauthorised disclosure). {#but-excludes-information-that-is-in-the-public-domain-other-than-by-way-of-unauthorised-disclosure. .unnumbered}

> **Group** means the Company and its subsidiaries, and **Group Company** means any one of them.
>
> **Moral Rights** has the meaning given in the *Copyright Act 1968* (Cth).
>
> **Works** means a literary, dramatic musical or artistic work.
