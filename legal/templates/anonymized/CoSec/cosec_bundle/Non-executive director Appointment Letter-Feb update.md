[PARTY] Resources Limited – Appointment as Non-Executive Director

We are pleased to confirm your appointment as a non-executive director of [PARTY] Resources Limited (the Company), commencing from [DATE], on the basis set out in this letter.

Your appointment as a non-executive director will be governed by the Constitution of the Company, the Corporations Act 2001 (Cth) (Corporations Act) and any other applicable legislation. 

In addition, you agree to act as a member of those committees of the board of the Company agreed between yourself and the board from time to time, and agree to comply with the terms of the relevant committee charters.

In consideration for your services, you will be paid a total fee of AUD [AMOUNT]per annum inclusive of statutory superannuation payments. There are no other entitlements other than these fees. These fees will accrue from the date of your appointment with payment to be made in line with the Company’s payment cycle, or as otherwise resolved by the board.

In addition, you will be eligible to participate in the Company’s employee securities incentive plan, subject to the plan’s terms and conditions and subject to shareholder approval, if required.

You will be entitled to be reimbursed, on presentation of accurate receipts, for all reasonable travel, accommodation or other expenses incurred as a result of you carrying out your duties for the Company.

The Company will obtain and maintain (on reasonable commercial terms) a directors’ and officers’ insurance policy.

You must carry out your duties as a non-executive director in accordance with Part 2D.1 of the Corporations Act, which sets out the duties and powers of directors of corporations, and the ASX listing rules.

You must keep the Company informed of your interests in securities and otherwise comply with all laws in the performance or your duties.

Your appointment as non-executive director may be terminated in accordance with the Constitution of the Company and the Corporations Act.

In addition to your obligations pursuant to the Constitution of the Company and the Corporations Act, both during and following the period of your appointment as a non-executive director of the Company, you must not disclose or use any confidential information of or relating to the Company except in the performance of your duties as a director of the Company. Confidential information includes board deliberations, financial information, internal reports and details of transactions or prospective transactions involving the Company, but does not include information available in the public domain. 

You will be an officeholder of the Company, but there will be no relationship of employer and employee as a consequence of your appointment as a non-executive director of the Company. 

You agree to comply with the Company’s policies as adopted by the board from time to time.

For our corporate records, please return a signed copy of this letter to us at your earliest convenience.

On behalf of the Company’s board of directors, we look forward to your contribution and service to the Company and look forward to working with you.
