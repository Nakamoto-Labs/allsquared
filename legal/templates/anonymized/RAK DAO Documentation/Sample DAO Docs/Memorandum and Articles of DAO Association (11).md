10. MEMORANDUM OF ASSOCIATION

    OF

    Solarakis DAO Limited

    A DAO ASSOCIATION

1.  Name

    The name of the DAO Association is Solarakis DAO Limited (the “DAO Association”), a DAO Association incorporated under the RAK Digital Assets Oasis DAO Association Regulations (the “Regulations”).

2.  Registered Office: [ADDRESS]
    Post Box #30099, RAKBANK Headquarters, Government of Ras Al Khaimah, United Arab Emirates'.

3.  Purpose of the DAO Association

    1.  The Purpose for which the DAO Association is established is:

        a.  Education DAOs;

        b.  to carry on all activities within the RAK Digital Assets Oasis (the “Zone”) as the RAK Digital Assets Oasis Authority (the “Authority”) may permit under the terms of the license issued in respect of the DAO Association; and

        c.  to carry on any other activities in connection with or as ancillary to the Purpose of the DAO Association as defined in this Clause ‎3(1).

    2.  The DAO Association shall not conduct any activities in the UAE outside the Zone (within the meaning of Article 5 of Federal Law No. 32 of 2021 concerning Commercial Companies) unless it has first obtained all appropriate licenses to conduct such activities from the competent authorities of the UAE and has complied with the applicable laws of the UAE.

4.  Duration of the DAO Association

    The duration of the DAO Association shall be twenty-five (25) years commencing on the date of the registration of the DAO Association in the DAO Association Register maintained by the Authority. Such period may be extended or shortened by a Governance Proposal at a General Meeting.

6.  DAO Association assets

    The assets of the DAO Association may comprise of any property that may be lawfully held by the DAO Association.

7.  Sub DAO Associations

    Subject to the Regulations, the DAO Association may register one or more Sub DAO Associations, up to a maximum of ten (10) Sub DAO Associations.

8.  Dividends

    1.  In this Clause ‎7, “dividend” means the distribution of retained revenue, representing profit, DAO Association to a Member.

    2.  The DAO Association shall not distribute a dividend to any Member.

    3.  The DAO Association shall re-invest or re-allocate any assets that represent retained revenue, representing profit, towards, an activity or activities that is or are consistent with the Purpose.

9.  Authorized Signatory

    1.  All of the documents, cheques and negotiable instruments, including the necessary documents to open, close and operate all DAO Association bank accounts, and all banking transactions, including credit and banking facilities for the DAO Association, may be signed by the Manager or Registered Agent following the receipt of approval from the Council.

    2.  The Council may at its discretion designate additional authorized signatories who are to hold the powers set out in Clause ‎9(1).

10. Accounts of the DAO Association

    1.  The DAO Association shall keep accounting records including underlying documents that are sufficient to show and explain its transactions so as to:

        a.  disclose with reasonable accuracy the financial position of the DAO Association at any time; and

        b.  enable the Council Members, Officer, Manager or Registered Agent to ensure that any accounts prepared by the DAO Association comply with the requirements of the Regulations.

    2.  A DAO Association’s accounting records and underlying documents shall be:

        a.  kept in an electronic format at such place as the Council believes is appropriate; and

        b.  preserved from the date on which they were created throughout the existence of the DAO Association and for five (5) years thereafter.

    3.  References to “Financial Year” in this Memorandum of Association are references to the first financial year and every subsequent financial year of the DAO Association as determined under this Clause ‎10.

    4.  The first financial year of the DAO Association shall commence on the date of incorporation of the DAO Association and shall be for a period of no less than six (6) months and no more than eighteen (18) months as determined by the DAO Association.

    5.  The subsequent financial year of a DAO Association shall be the period of twelve (12) months commencing with the end of the first financial year of the DAO Association as calculated and notified to the Registrar under Clause ‎10(4) above.

    6.  Every subsequent financial year of the DAO Association shall be the period of twelve (12) months commencing with the end of the previous financial year.

    7.  The DAO Association may specify a new financial year provided that this will not cause any financial year of the DAO Association, whether past, present or future, to exceed eighteen (18) months in duration or be shorter than six (6) months.

    8.  The Council shall arrange for the preparation of the DAO Association’s balance sheet and profit and loss account and shall also prepare an annual report of the DAO Association’s activities, its financial position and the proposal for the re-investment or re-allocation of any assets that represent retained revenue, representing profit, all to be completed within six (6) months from the end of the DAO Association’s Financial Year.

11. Auditor

  The Council shall, following the passing of a Governance Proposal, appoint a firm of auditors (the “auditor”).

12. Security Interests

    1.  A Member may pledge all or any of the Tokens owned by them to a bank, financial institution or other lender in respect of a debt or obligation of the Member, the DAO Association or any other person, by virtue of a written instrument, signed by the Member, in a form approved by the Registrar.

    2.  The Council may approve the DAO Association granting as security for a debt or obligation of the DAO Association or any other person any of the general assets held by the DAO Association in favour of any person.

13. Winding Up

    1.  The DAO Association may be wound up voluntarily where:

        a.  it is resolved by a Governance Proposal that the DAO Association is to be wound up; and

        b.  one or more of the following conditions are met at the time of the passing of the Governance Proposal:

            i.  [the expiry of the duration provided for in this Memorandum of Association;]

            ii. where this Memorandum of Association provides for an expiration event, circumstance or condition, when that event, circumstance or condition occurs;

            iii. the DAO Association is not able to appoint any further Council Members for any reason, including a shortage of appropriate candidates; or

            iv. the Council has passed a decision that the Purpose of the DAO Association has been achieved to its reasonable satisfaction.

    2.  Where the DAO Association is to be wound up voluntarily, the Council shall appoint one or more auditors as liquidators by a Governance Proposal.

14. Amendment of the Memorandum of Association

    1.  With the exception of amendments to Clause ‎3 of this Memorandum of Association, this Memorandum of Association may be amended by a Governance Proposal.

    2.  A proposed amendment to Clause ‎3 of this Memorandum of Association shall only have effect subject to the following:

        a.  the unanimous approval of the proposed amendment by the Council;

        b.  the passing of a Governance Proposal approving the proposed amendment, with no fewer than 75% of the persons voting on the Governance Proposal by number voting in favor; and

        c.  the issuance of the required regulatory approvals.

15. Governing Law

  This Memorandum of Association and all rights and obligations of the Members and Officers of the DAO Association shall be governed and construed in accordance with the laws, rules, and regulations of the Authority and the applicable laws of Abu Dhabi Global Market (ADGM). The courts of Abu Dhabi Global Market shall have exclusive jurisdiction to settle any dispute arising out of or in connection with this Memorandum of Association.

16. Notices

    1.  A notice sent by the DAO Association to a Member shall only be deemed to have been given when the notice has been delivered to the Electronic address specified by that Member.

    2.  Where no Electronic address has been specified by a Member, a Notice sent by the DAO Association to the Member shall be deemed to have been given when it is posted to an Electronic forum, channel or platform to which the Member has access or had access at the time at which the notice was sent.

17. Transfer of Incorporation

  The DAO Association may, by a Governance Proposal, and following the receipt of all necessary regulatory approvals, apply to the appropriate official or public body of a foreign jurisdiction to transfer its incorporation to the foreign jurisdiction and request that the DAO Association be continued as a foreign DAO Association.

18. Miscellaneous

    1.  Where a term has not been defined in this Memorandum of Association, it shall have the meaning provided in the Regulations.

    2.  The headings in this Memorandum of Association are for convenience only and shall not affect its interpretation.

    3.  Where the context so requires, the singular shall include the plural and words in the masculine gender shall include the feminine gender and/or neutral gender and vice-versa.

    4.  The DAO Association shall not perform any activities until it is registered in the DAO Association Register of the Authority.

    5.  Matters not provided for in this Memorandum of Association shall be subject to the provisions of the Regulations and any other applicable Emirate of RAK or UAE law.

    6.  This Memorandum of Association and any amendments thereto shall be written in English language.

    7.  Where any amendments are made to this Memorandum of Association, such amendments shall be annexed thereto.

    8.  In the event of any dispute with respect to any clause of this Memorandum of Association, the matter shall be brought to the Court.

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, the Founding Members have signed this Memorandum of Association on the 17th day of October, 2024.
