**[Company Name]**
**Effective Date:** [Date]
**Version:** 1.0

## 1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes guidelines for the responsible use of artificial intelligence (AI) and generative AI tools by [Company Name] employees, contractors, and agents. It aims to maximize the benefits of AI while managing risks to the organization, its clients, and the public.

## 2. SCOPE

This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and temporary workers
- All AI tools, including but not limited to: large language models (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, etc.), image generators, code assistants, AI-powered analytics, and any AI features embedded in existing software
- Both company-approved and personal AI tools used for work purposes

## 4. PERMITTED USES

AI tools may be used for:
- [ ] Drafting and editing written content (with human review)
- [ ] Code generation and debugging (with human review and testing)
- [ ] Research and information gathering (with source verification)
- [ ] Data analysis and visualization
- [ ] Translation and localization
- [ ] Meeting summaries and note-taking
- [ ] Brainstorming and ideation
- [ ] Customer service assistance (with human oversight)
- [ ] Internal process automation

## 5. PROHIBITED USES

AI tools must NOT be used to:

### 5.1 Data Security
- Input confidential, proprietary, or trade secret information into non-approved AI tools
- Input personal data (PII/PHI) of customers, employees, or third parties without proper safeguards
- Input financial data, credentials, API keys, or security-sensitive information
- Upload internal documents, source code, or databases to public AI services

### 5.2 Decision-Making
- Make final hiring, firing, promotion, or compensation decisions without human review
- Make credit, lending, insurance, or benefits decisions without human oversight
- Make legal or medical determinations without professional review
- Profile individuals based on protected characteristics

### 5.3 Content and Representation
- Represent AI-generated content as original human work in situations where disclosure is required
- Generate deepfakes, synthetic media, or deceptive content
- Use AI to impersonate individuals or create misleading communications
- Submit AI-generated work product to clients without disclosure (where contractually required)

### 5.4 Legal and Ethical
- Circumvent content filters, safety mechanisms, or usage restrictions
- Use AI for surveillance of employees or customers beyond disclosed purposes
- Use AI in ways that violate applicable laws, regulations, or contractual obligations
- Use AI to gain unauthorized access to systems or data

## 6. DATA HANDLING REQUIREMENTS

### 6.1 Classification Before Input
Before inputting any data into an AI tool, employees must classify the data:
- **Public** → May use with approved AI tools
- **Internal** → May use only with Tier 1 approved tools
- **Confidential** → May use only with enterprise-licensed tools with appropriate DPA
- **Restricted/Regulated** → Prohibited from AI input without CISO approval

### 6.2 Personal Data
- Never input PII/PHI into AI tools without a valid DPA/BAA in place
- Anonymize or pseudonymize data before AI processing where possible
- Comply with GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, and other applicable data protection laws

## 7. QUALITY ASSURANCE

### 7.1 Human Review
All AI outputs must be reviewed by a qualified human before:
- External communication to clients, partners, or regulators
- Publication or distribution
- Use in decision-making
- Incorporation into deliverables

### 7.2 Fact-Checking
- AI-generated facts, statistics, citations, and references must be independently verified
- AI-generated legal, medical, financial, or compliance advice must be reviewed by qualified professionals
- AI-generated code must be tested and reviewed before deployment

### 7.3 Bias Awareness
- Be aware that AI systems may reflect biases in their training data
- Review AI outputs for potential bias, particularly in hiring, lending, and customer-facing applications
- Report suspected bias to [designated contact]

## 8. DISCLOSURE AND TRANSPARENCY

### 8.1 Internal Disclosure
- Employees should disclose significant AI assistance in work product as appropriate
- AI-generated content should be marked or disclosed per departmental guidelines

### 8.2 External Disclosure
- Client contracts may require disclosure of AI use — check before using
- Regulatory filings may require disclosure — consult Legal
- Marketing and public communications: follow brand guidelines on AI disclosure

## 9. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 9.1 Ownership Considerations
- AI-generated outputs may not qualify for copyright protection in all jurisdictions
- Inputs to AI tools may be used for training (check tool terms of service)
- Enterprise licenses with training opt-outs are preferred for sensitive work

### 9.2 Third-Party IP
- Do not input third-party copyrighted material for purposes of generating derivative works
- Review AI outputs for potential IP infringement before use
- Do not use AI to circumvent DRM or licensing restrictions

## 10. INCIDENT REPORTING

Report the following to [IT Security / Compliance]:
- Accidental input of confidential data into unauthorized AI tools
- Discovery of biased or harmful AI outputs
- Suspected AI security incidents
- Potential regulatory violations involving AI

## 11. TRAINING AND AWARENESS

- All employees must complete AI awareness training within [30] days of this policy's effective date
- Annual refresher training is required
- Department-specific AI training may be required for specialized use cases

## 12. COMPLIANCE AND ENFORCEMENT

- Intentional violations involving customer data or regulatory obligations may result in legal action
