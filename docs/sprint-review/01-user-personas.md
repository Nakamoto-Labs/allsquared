## Overview: Two-Sided Market

AllSquared serves **two distinct user types** with different pain points, motivations, and behaviors:

1. **Freelancers/Service Providers** (supply side) - Need payment protection
2. **Clients** (demand side) - Need quality assurance and scope control

Success requires BOTH sides to see value. This document defines 3 primary personas (2 freelancers, 1 client) to guide product and marketing decisions.

## Key Insights for Product & Marketing

### 1. **Two-sided messaging required**
- **For freelancers**: "Get paid on time, every time"
- **For clients**: "Only pay for work you approve"
- **Shared value**: "Escrow protects both sides"

### 2. **Free tier is essential (for freelancers)**
Tom and Sarah both need to test before committing. Free tier drives viral adoption (client receives contract, sees AllSquared, signs up).

### 3. **Client acquisition is the bottleneck**
Freelancers can't force clients to use AllSquared. Must market to BOTH sides equally.

David represents the "hard sell" - he needs education on why escrow helps HIM, not just the freelancer.

### 4. **UX bar is HIGH**
Sarah compares to HoneyBook (beautiful UX). David compares to Upwork (seamless escrow). We must match or exceed.

### 5. **Price sensitivity varies**
- Tom: ¬£30/month = no-brainer (vs ¬£12K loss)
- Sarah: ¬£20/month = threshold (family budget matters)
- David: 2.5% escrow fee = cheap (vs Upwork 20%)

**Recommendation**: Offer ¬£19.99 tier for Sarah, ¬£29.99 for Tom, or dynamic pricing based on project value.
