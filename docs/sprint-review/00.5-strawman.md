## Deliberate Provocations

### 1. **"We should charge clients, not freelancers"**

**Current plan**: Freelancer pays Â£29.99/month subscription.

**Provocation**: What if the client pays the escrow fee instead?

**Why**: 
- Clients care more about "only paying for good work" than freelancers care about "getting paid on time"
- Removes friction for freelancer adoption (free for them)
- Client has budget (especially enterprise), freelancer is cost-sensitive
- Makes virality easier (freelancer invites client, client pays)

**Counterargument**: Clients might balk at "another fee." But 2.5% is cheaper than chargebacks, legal disputes, or hiring a bad freelancer.

### 2. **"Launch with ZERO AI contract generation"**

**Current plan**: AI-powered contract generation is the headline feature.

**Provocation**: Ship V1 with 5 static templates. Add AI in V1.1 after 100 customers.

**Why**:
- AI contracts are unvalidated (do they actually hold up in disputes?)
- Static templates are faster to build, less risky legally
- Customers care about escrow MORE than AI (solve the money problem first)
- AI is expensive (Â£0.10/contract adds up fast at scale)

**Counterargument**: "AI-powered" is a marketing hook. But if the AI generates bad contracts, it's worse than no AI.

### 3. **"Target trade contractors (plumbers, builders) FIRST, not digital freelancers"**

**Current plan**: Primary = digital freelancers, Secondary = trades (Phase 2).

**Provocation**: Flip it. Target trades first.

**Why**:
- Trades have HIGHER payment risk (Â£10K-50K jobs, material costs upfront)
- Trades are LESS tech-savvy (fewer competitors, lower expectations)
- Trades have EXISTING escrow demand (TrustMark, Checkatrade already doing it)
- Digital freelancers have HoneyBook/Bonsai already - trades have nothing

**Counterargument**: Trades are harder to reach online (not on Reddit/Twitter). But they're on Facebook, local groups, Checkatrade.

## What's WRONG with the Current Framing?

### 1. **"AI-powered" is the hook, but escrow is the moat**
The business plan leads with "AI contract generation" but buries "FCA-backed escrow" as a feature. **This is backwards.** 

- HoneyBook can add AI tomorrow (they probably already have)
- FCA-regulated escrow takes 12 months and Â£500K in compliance
- Lead with the moat, not the feature

**Fix**: Rebrand as "Escrow-first contracts" not "AI-first contracts."

### 2. **Four pricing tiers is three too many**
Free / Â£9.99 / Â£29.99 / Â£99.99 is SaaS orthodoxy, but it's wrong for this product.

- Free tier is fine (virality)
- Â£9.99 tier attracts people who won't use escrow (wrong customers)
- Â£99.99 tier is enterprise (not ready for that complexity)

**Fix**: Free + Â£29.99. That's it. Test for 90 days, then add tiers if needed.

### 3. **"10M potential users" is a vanity metric**
The business plan claims "10M+ potential users in UK" but that's everyone who's ever freelanced. The REAL TAM is:

- Freelancers earning Â£50K+/year (250K people)
- Who do Â£5K+ contracts (not Â£500 gigs)
- Who experience payment anxiety (maybe 50K)

**Fix**: Target TAM is 50K, not 10M. Focus beats scale in year 1.

### 4. **No plan for what happens when HoneyBook enters UK**
The competitive analysis assumes US tools stay US-focused. But HoneyBook has $250M in funding. If AllSquared shows traction, HoneyBook just adds UK compliance and crushes us.

**Fix**: The moat is NOT "UK-first." The moat is escrow + speed. Build defensibility around regulatory compliance and trust.

## Who's the REAL User?

**The business plan says**: "UK freelancers."

**The strawman says**: "Freelancers who experienced financial loss in the past 12 months."

**The REAL answer**: **Both the freelancer AND the client** (two-sided market).

### Why This Matters
AllSquared is sold as a "freelancer tool" but it only works if CLIENTS adopt it too. This is Uber's problem: drivers want rides, passengers want drivers. You need both.

**Current GTM focuses 90% on freelancers, 10% on clients. Should be 50/50.**

Messaging needs to answer:
- **For freelancers**: "Get paid on time, even if the client ghosts"
- **For clients**: "Only pay for work you approve. Your money is protected too."

## What Should We REMOVE?
