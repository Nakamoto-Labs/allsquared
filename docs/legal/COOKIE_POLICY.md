## 2. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to:

- Make websites work efficiently
- Remember your preferences
- Improve user experience
- Provide analytics and insights

### 2.1 Types of Storage Technologies

| Technology | Description |
|------------|-------------|
| **Cookies** | Small text files stored by your browser |
| **Local Storage** | Larger data storage in your browser |
| **Session Storage** | Temporary storage cleared when browser closes |
| **Pixels/Web Beacons** | Small images that track page views |

## 3. How We Use Cookies

These cookies are essential for the Platform to function. They cannot be disabled.

| Cookie Name | Provider | Purpose | Duration |

## 5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

### 5.1 Cookie Banner

When you first visit our Platform, you'll see a cookie banner allowing you to:

- **Accept All:** Enable all cookie categories
- **Reject Non-Essential:** Only allow strictly necessary cookies
- **Customise:** Choose specific cookie categories

### 5.2 Changing Preferences

You can change your preferences at any time by:

1. Clicking the cookie icon in the footer
2. Going to Settings > Privacy > Cookie Preferences
3. Contacting us at privacy@allsquared.co.uk

### 5.3 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to control cookies through settings:

- **Chrome:** Settings > Privacy and security > Cookies
- **Firefox:** Options > Privacy & Security > Cookies
- **Safari:** Preferences > Privacy
- **Edge:** Settings > Privacy, search, and services

**Note:** Blocking all cookies may affect Platform functionality.

### 5.4 Opt-Out Links

You can opt out of specific third-party tracking:

- **Google Analytics:** [tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout)
- **Facebook:** [facebook.com/ads/preferences](https://www.facebook.com/ads/preferences)
- **LinkedIn:** [linkedin.com/psettings/advertising](https://www.linkedin.com/psettings/advertising)

## 6. Do Not Track

Some browsers send a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. There is no industry standard for responding to DNT signals, so we currently do not respond to them. However, you can opt out of tracking using the methods above.
